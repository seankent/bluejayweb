Bluejay FPGA Development Board

Overview

The Bluejay is a low-power FPGA development board built around the iCE40LP8K from Lattice Semiconductor.

The iCE40LP8K is part of the iCE40 LP/HX family of ultra-low-power FPGAs. It is the largest FPGA in the family, featuring 7,680 Look-Up Tables (LUTs), 128 Kbits of Embedded Block RAM, and two Phase-Locked Loops for on-chip clock generation. Its combination of logic capacity and energy efficiency makes it well-suited for battery-powered and energy-constrained applications.

The Bluejay provides a convenient, flexible platform for working with the iCE40LP8K. The board includes 39 digital I/O pins, a 12 MHz oscillator, and a 2 Mbit flash chip for persistent bitstream storage. The board's I/O operates at 3.3V logic levels, though the board can be powered from either a 5V or 3.3V supply. When idle, the board consumes just 2 mW of power.

The board maximizes the logic and I/O resources available to the designer while keeping onboard features to a minimum. Additional functionality, such as sensors or displays, can be connected through the board's I/O pins as needed. This approach allows designers to include only what's required for their specific application, keeping designs lean and power-efficient.

The Bluejay works seamlessly with completely free and open-source software that runs on Linux, macOS, and Windows. This includes a complete RTL-to-bitstream toolchain as well as tools for programming the FPGA and onboard flash.
