Board I/O

Header Pins

The Bluejay provides access to 39 of the iCE40's Programmable I/O (PIO) pins. These pins are exposed through two 24-pin headers, J1 and J2, and are labeled D0-D38. Each PIO can be configured as an input, output, or tri-state buffer.

In addition to the PIO pins, the board includes a configuration interface for programming the iCE40 and flash chip. This is done through a SPI interface (CS, SCK, MOSI, and MISO) and two dedicated iCE40 configuration pins, CRST and CDN.

The J1 and J2 headers also include a handful of 3V3 and GND pins, as well as a 5V pin. Depending on the power delivery method, these pins can act as either power inputs or outputs.

Aside from the 5V power pin, all of the board's I/O operates at a 3.3 V logic level.

Pin Mapping

As mentioned above, the Bluejay breaks out 39 of the iCE40's PIO pins, which are connected to header pins D0-D38. When programming the iCE40, the top-level inputs and outputs of the RTL design must be assigned to physical pins on the FPGA package. This information is specified in a pin constraints file (.pcf) and is fed into the place-and-route tool as part of the bitstream generation flow.

When creating a .pcf file, it is important to know how the iCE40 pins are connected to the board's header pins. This mapping is provided in the table below.

For reference, the specific chip used on the Bluejay is the iCE40LP8K-CM81, which comes in 81-ball ucBGA package. The balls are laid out in a 9x9 grid array, with each ball designated by a row letter, "A" through "J" (the letter "I" is skipped), and a column number.

Note, the connections between the MOSI and MISO pins and the iCE40 depend on the configuration mode. In Master Mode, MOSI is connected to G6 (IOB_105_SDO) and MISO is connect to H7 (IOB_106_SDI). In Slave Mode, these connections are reversed.

12 MHz Clock Pin Mapping

In addition to the header pin connections listed in the table above, one other pin mapping is of particular relavance. This is the connection between the iCE40 and the board's 12 MHz oscillator, which provides a clock signal for the FPGA. The 12 MHz square wave output of the oscillator is connected to ball C4 (IOT_198_GBIN0) of the FPGA.
