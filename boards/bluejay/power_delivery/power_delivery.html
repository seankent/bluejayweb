Power Delivery

Overview

The Bluejay primarily operates at a 3.3 V logic level. This includes the iCE40’s I/O, flash chip, and oscillator. All header pins (exepct the 5V pin) operate at 3.3V.

To accommodate a variety of use cases, the board can be powered from either 3.3 V or 5 V supply. When powered from a 5 V supply, an onboard LDO steps the input voltage down to 3.3 V. A second LDO steps the 3.3 V supply down to 1.2 V for the iCE40’s core logic.

The board can be powered from one of the 3V3 or 5V pins, or through the USB-C connector. Each of these power delivery option are described in more detail in the following sections.

Power Though the 3V3 Pins

The board can be powered directly from on of the 3V3 pin. This allows the board to be easily integrated into larger systems that already have a regulated 3.3 V supply. It also simplifies the configuration setup by allowing the board and the host programmer to operate off of the same supply.

When powering the board from a 3V3 pin, the 5V pin and the USB-C connector should be disconnected.

Power Though the 5V Pins

The board can also be powered from the 5V pin. This can be a convenient option when only a 5 V supply is available. The 5 V input is stepped down to 3.3 V by an LDO for use by the rest of the board.

When powering the board from the 5V pin, the 3V3 pins are driven from the ouput of the LDO and can be used as power outputs. The USB-C connetor should be disconnected.

Power Though the USB-C Connector

In addition to the 3V3 and 5V pins, the board can be powered from the USB-C connector. The USB-C connector provides the board with a 5 V supply.

When power is supplied through the USB-C connector, both the 5V and 3V3 pins can be used as power outputs (at their respective voltages).

Note, the USB-C connector is for power only. Data cannot be transferred to or from the device through the connector.
